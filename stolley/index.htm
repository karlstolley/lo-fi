Preamble

In the rush and compulsion to be bleeding edge, digital producers have too often chosen the newer, flashier method of working while neglecting to explore, to any depth, older established technologies that can often better serve the aims and purpose of digital discourse artifacts—especially for the Web.

Lo-fi production technologies are rooted in two basic things: one, the plain text file and its manifestations like XML, XHTML, CSS, JavaScript, and PHP and two, the single-media file in widely used, preferably open, formats: images as JPEG and PNG; sound as MP3; video as MPEG.

The champions of lo-fi production are the text file and the single-media file.

Definitions

L.O.-F.I.

  • Lossless: Discourse presented through lo-fi production technologies neither degrades nor becomes trapped in the production itself. Text migrates and transforms; images, video, and other media maintain their integrity.
  • Open: Lo-fi production technologies make their source code and materials available for inspection, revision, and extension outside of the scope of any one piece of production software. Aside from single-media files for image, video, and sound,
  • Flexible: Content presented using lo-fi production technologies can be experienced unobstrusively in different ways by different users.
  • In(ter)dependent: Lo-fi production technologies direct orchestration (a recipe), not composition (a TV dinner), allowing users’ devices to interpret the production the way a chef interprets a recipe
